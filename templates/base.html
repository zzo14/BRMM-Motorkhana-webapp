What is a Motorkhana?

A timed driver skill competition - there are usually 6 tests of up to 200m in length defined by cones and starting and finishing garages. Maps are provided showing the direction of travel and the course to be followed around each cone. The objective is to complete the course in the correct direction without incurring penalties, such as 5 seconds for hitting a cone or leaving the sealed surface, in the fastest time.

Run in a safe environment on our grassed areas, these events are a challenge even for the most experienced. To drive a course around up to 25 cones in an area no larger than 200m x200m (often less) requires skill. Sliding the car, using the handbrake to turn, and controlling acceleration is essential to be neat and fast, while avoiding those pesky 5-second time penalty cones. A standard event runs 6x courses with each driver getting 2 attempts at each. Some courses even include reversing!
